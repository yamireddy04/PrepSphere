Cold Emailing

Reach the right people — even without an open door

Cold emailing is the practice of reaching out to a professional — usually a hiring manager or recruiter — without prior interaction. It's typically done to express interest in job opportunities or internships at a company, even when no job has been publicly posted.

› When to Use

  • ✔️To inquire about hidden job opportunities
  • ✔️To introduce yourself as a potential candidate
  • ✔️To expand your network in a targeted company or field

› Structure of a Cold Email

  • ✔️Subject Line: Clear and professional
  • ✔️Introduction: Who you are and why you are reaching out
  • ✔️Value Proposition: What makes you a good fit
  • ✔️Call to Action: Request for a reply, meeting, or consideration

Sample Template

Sample Cold Email 
Subject: Aspiring Software Developer Seeking Opportunities at [Company Name]

Dear [Hiring Manager's Name],

I hope this message finds you well. My name is [Name], a [Education, College]. I'm writing to express my deep interest in potential software development roles at [Company Name], known for its innovation in [industry/field].

I specialize in full-stack development using Angular and Python, and recently developed a job-prep platform named PrepSphere. I am confident that my technical skills and passion for problem-solving would add value to your team.

Please find my resume attached. I would be grateful for an opportunity to discuss how I might contribute to your organization.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Warm regards,
Name
[Email] | [LinkedIn profile]

Pro Tips

Personalise Every Email

Mention their name, role, or a recent company milestone — generic emails get ignored.

Keep It Concise

Aim for 3–4 short paragraphs. Busy professionals won't read walls of text.

Follow Up Once

If no reply in 7–10 days, send one polite follow-up. Don't chase further.
