LinkedIn Messaging

Direct outreach that opens real conversations

Direct messaging on LinkedIn involves sending short, personalized messages to HRs or recruiters to initiate a conversation about job openings or opportunities. Done right, it signals initiative and professionalism before you even submit a resume.

› When to Use

  • ✔️After seeing a job posting on LinkedIn
  • ✔️To express interest in a specific team or role
  • ✔️To follow up on a submitted application

› Best Practices

  • ✔️Keep it concise and professional
  • ✔️Avoid sending your resume immediately unless asked
  • ✔️Always personalise — mention their role, company, or a recent post

Sample Template

Sample LinkedIn Message 
Hi [Recruiter's Name],

I hope you're doing well. I'm [Name], [Education], and I am interested in [type] roles at [Company Name]. I recently came across your profile and wanted to connect to learn more about openings in your team.

I have worked on projects involving Angular, Python, and AI-based tools. I am confident my skills in full-stack development and my passion for creating efficient solutions would be a great asset.

Looking forward to connecting!

Best,
Name

What Makes a Great DM

Open With Context

Mention how you found them — a shared post, a job listing, or a mutual connection. It sets a natural tone.

One Clear Ask

End with a single, specific request — a 15-minute call, a referral, or just to connect. Don't overwhelm with questions.

Stay Under 150 Words

LinkedIn DMs should be scannable. Short, crisp messages get replies far more often than long pitches.
