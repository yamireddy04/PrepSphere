Emailing After Rejection

Turn a closed door into a future opportunity

A follow-up message sent after a rejection may seem counterintuitive, but it demonstrates maturity and professionalism — and keeps you in consideration for future openings. It's one of the most underused strategies in a job seeker's toolkit.

› When to Use

  • ✔️After receiving a rejection email
  • ✔️If you had progressed through multiple interview rounds
  • ✔️When you want to express continued interest in the company

› Why It Matters

  • ✔️Shows maturity and professionalism
  • ✔️Keeps the door open for future roles
  • ✔️Sometimes leads to reconsideration or referrals

Sample Template

Sample Email After Rejection 
Subject: Thank You — Continued Interest in Opportunities at [Company Name]

Dear [Recruiter Name],

Thank you for informing me about the decision regarding the [Job Title] position. While I am naturally disappointed, I am grateful for the opportunity to have interviewed with [Company Name].

I genuinely enjoyed our conversations and learning more about your team and projects. I remain very interested in your work and would love to be considered for any future openings that align with my skills.

Thank you once again for your time and feedback.

Warm regards,
Name
[Email] | [LinkedIn]

The Right Mindset

Send Within 24–48 Hours

Timing matters. A prompt, gracious reply while you're still fresh in their memory is far more impactful.

Ask for Feedback

Politely request any pointers on how you could improve. Most recruiters appreciate the growth mindset — some will actually reply.

No Bitterness, Ever

Keep the tone warm and forward-looking. Industries are smaller than you think — impressions last beyond a single role.
