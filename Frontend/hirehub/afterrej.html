3. Emailing After Rejection

What It Is:
This is a follow-up message sent to a recruiter after being rejected for a role. While it may seem counterintuitive, it demonstrates professionalism and keeps you in consideration for future openings.

When to Use:

  • After receiving a rejection mail
  • If you had progressed through multiple interview rounds
  • When you want to express continued interest in the company

Why It Matters:

  • Shows maturity and professionalism
  • Keeps the door open for future roles
  • Sometimes leads to reconsideration or referrals
Sample Email After Rejection 
Subject: Thank You , Continued Interest in Opportunities at [Company Name]

Dear [Recruiter Name],

Thank you for informing me about the decision regarding the [Job Title] position. While I am naturally disappointed, I am grateful for the opportunity to have interviewed with [Company Name].

I genuinely enjoyed our conversations and learning more about your team and projects. I remain very interested in your work and would love to be considered for any future openings that align with my skills.

Thank you once again for your time and feedback.

Warm regards,  
Name
