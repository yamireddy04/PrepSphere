Section 01
Strategy

Job Search Strategies

Cast your net wide — but deliberately. Every channel has its advantage. Knowing which one to hit first, and how, is what separates active candidates from those just waiting to get lucky.

Digital
Online Job Boards

LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor — search with precision filters and set alerts so the best roles reach you first.

Direct
Company Careers Pages

Apply directly before the role hits the aggregators. Early applications carry disproportionate weight.

Relationships
Network Strategically

Build genuine connections. Referrals unlock roles that never get posted publicly — and they convert far better.

In-Person
Industry Events

Hackathons, career expos, and meetups put you face-to-face with the people who make hiring decisions.

Section 02
Execution

Apply Effectively

Volume is not strategy. Every application you send should be crafted to win — not merely to be submitted. Precision and persistence together are what move the needle.

Precision
Tailor Every Application

Customize resume and cover letter per role. Relevant over comprehensive — always.

Intel
Research Thoroughly

Know the company's mission, culture, and recent news before you write a single word of your application.

Visibility
Maintain Online Presence

Keep LinkedIn sharp. Engage with your industry publicly. Recruiters are watching before you apply.

Follow-Up
Follow Up Thoughtfully

A brief, well-timed follow-up signals professionalism — not desperation. Time it right.

Pro Tips — Stay Sharp
01
Practice Interview Questions

Know your common technical and HR questions cold before each round — no surprises.

02
Conduct Mock Interviews

Simulate with friends or mentors. Confidence is built in practice, not in the moment.

03
Stay Persistent

Every rejection is data. Refine your approach, keep moving. Momentum is its own advantage.

04
Keep Learning

Certifications, side projects, and courses signal growth. Stay sharper than the competition.

"Stay proactive. Stay resilient. You will land exactly where you're meant to be."
